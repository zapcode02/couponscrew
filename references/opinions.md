# Opinions & Hot Takes

> Couponscrew has strong, specific opinions about online shopping and the coupon industry.
> These are what separate real deal-hunter content from generic listicles.
> Always back an opinion with a number or a real example — "bank card offers save money" is nothing. A rupee amount makes it an opinion.

---

## On the coupon industry

- **Most coupon sites list expired codes and don't care.** It's a traffic play — they rank for "brand coupon code", you land, you try five dead codes, you leave frustrated. We verify before we publish. That's the only meaningful difference between a good coupon site and a directory of disappointments.
- **"Exclusive coupon" usually means nothing.** Every coupon site calls their codes exclusive. A code is exclusive when it genuinely cannot be found elsewhere — which is rare. We'll say it when it's true.
- **Aggregator sites that show 47 codes for a brand — all expired — are actively harmful.** They waste time and make people distrust coupons entirely. Two verified codes beat 47 dead ones every time.
- **Browser extension cashback apps are the most underused savings tool in India.** Stack one with a coupon code on a sale day and you're using every layer available. Most shoppers don't even know the layer exists.

## On how Indians actually shop online

- **Cashback drives Indian shopping decisions more than discounts.** 50% of Indians shop online because of cashback offers vs 46% for product discounts (Mintel). Write about cashback like it matters — because it does.
- **Tier-2 and Tier-3 cities are where the growth is.** Tier-3 cities posted 21% YoY e-commerce growth in 2025 (Redseer). Content that speaks to shoppers outside metros is underserved and undervalued.
- **The real Big Billion Day deal window is the first 4 hours.** After that, most top items are sold out or back to near-normal prices. Set an alarm or miss it — there's no polite way to say it.
- **Bank card offers are where the real savings stack.** An HDFC or SBI offer on top of a sale price plus a coupon code can bring a ₹5,000 purchase down to ₹3,000. Most shoppers use one layer. Smart shoppers use three.
- **"No cost EMI" is not free.** The processing fee and the interest are often baked into the product price. Always compare the outright price to the EMI total before celebrating.
- **App-only deals exist to get the app installed.** That's fine — install the app, use the deal, keep it if it's useful. Don't keep seven apps permanently installed just for one-time coupons though.
- **Wishlist + price drop notification = the most underused feature in Indian e-commerce.** Add it, forget it, get notified when it drops. Works especially well on Flipkart and Amazon.

## On specific platforms

- **Swiggy vs Zomato promo codes rotate constantly.** What works today may not work tomorrow. Never bookmark a food delivery code — bookmark the page where we track them in real time.
- **Myntra's End of Reason Sale discounts are real.** Unlike some platform sales, EORS typically has genuine markdowns, not inflated-MRP tricks. Worth planning a list for.
- **Amazon Great Indian Festival vs Flipkart Big Billion Days:** GIF tends to be stronger on electronics, BBD tends to be stronger on fashion and appliances. India's festive season e-commerce hit $14 billion GMV in 2024 (Redseer) — these sales are real events, not marketing theatre.
- **Hostinger India deals are legitimately among the best in web hosting globally.** The 80–85% off deals are real — deep discounting to acquire long-term customers. The catch: renewal prices are higher. Factor that in upfront.
- **Quick commerce (Blinkit, Zepto, Instamart) is the fastest-growing category.** The segment hit $7–8 billion in FY25 and is growing at 110–130% CAGR (Redseer). Coupon content for quick commerce is currently massively undersupplied vs demand.

## On saving strategy

- **Stack everything.** Sale price + coupon code + bank card offer + cashback app. Every layer counts and they're all legal.
- **First-order discounts are a one-time thing — use them on your biggest purchase, not a trial order.** A 60% off first order is worth ₹600 on a ₹1,000 order. It's worth ₹1,800 on a ₹3,000 order. Basic maths, easy to miss.
- **Refer-and-earn programmes are worth doing once.** After that, you're doing unpaid marketing for a brand for ₹50. Decide if that's your time.
- **Price tracking before any purchase above ₹3,000 is non-negotiable.** Prices move more than people realise. Check the 90-day history before assuming a "sale" price is actually a low price.

## How to use opinions in content

- **One strong opinion per blog post, max.** Use it where it makes the post feel human instead of generic.
- **Always back an opinion with a number or a real example.**
- **Tell readers when NOT to bother with a coupon.** If a code has too many restrictions to be useful, say so. This is the biggest trust signal — generic content never does it.
